How to Use

  1. Take a photo from the spot where you plan to mount a security camera. Use your phone's ultrawide (0.5x) lens for the widest view.
  2. Upload the photo and select your phone model and lens/zoom used.
  3. Calibrate distances by placing at least 2 horizontal markers on features at known distances (e.g., sidewalk edge at 15ft, far curb at 40ft). Closer and farther markers give better results.
  4. Add cameras from the presets or enter custom specs. Each camera gets a colored overlay.
  5. Read the visualization:
    • Green zone (≥40 PPF): Can identify individuals — faces recognizable
    • Yellow zone (20–40 PPF): Can recognize known people — general features visible
    • Orange zone (10–20 PPF): Can detect presence — motion and shapes visible
    • The mini-map shows a top-down view of each camera's full FOV
  6. Use Compare Cameras to see a side-by-side stats table.

Note: Distance calibration assumes roughly flat ground and a level camera. PPF values for ultra-wide (180°) cameras are averaged across the frame — center regions will have higher PPF than edges.
